# On-Device STT: Frontier Survey vs. Murmur Rebuild (Plan 06)

**Date:** 2026-07-04 · **Researcher:** sonnet worker · **Commissioned by:** dam via team-lead
**Purpose:** map the on-device speech-to-text frontier (mid-2026) against Murmur's hard requirements — fully on-device, hour+ background capture on a locked phone at a job site, streaming transcript feeding a Rust extraction cursor that assumes **append-only** text, and a ≤100-term curated vocabulary that must bias recognition — to inform the Rust-side-vs-platform-side architectural fork for Plan 06.

**Caveat up front:** search results are dated mid-2026 and include vendor blogs, one arXiv preprint (Moonshine v2) with a 2026 identifier, and several SEO-adjacent comparison sites (dicta.to, spokenly.app, vexascribe/novascribe) whose benchmark methodology is not independently audited. I've flagged confidence per claim. Nothing here should be treated as load-bearing until we run our own benchmark (see final section).

---

## Landscape (condensed, by engine)

- **Apple SpeechAnalyzer / SpeechTranscriber (iOS 26+, WWDC25/2025-06)**: new proprietary on-device model, modular concurrency-native API. Removes SFSpeechRecognizer's ~1-minute session cap for long-form audio; runs fully on-device; language assets downloaded via system asset catalog (not app-bundled). Apple claims ~2× faster than Whisper Large-v3-Turbo on equivalent tasks (Apple, WWDC25 — **unverified independently**). Argmax's own benchmark (2025-06-20, M4 Mac mini, macOS 26 beta) put it at 14.0% WER / 70x speed-factor on earnings22 vs WhisperKit small.en at 12.8%/35x and Argmax Pro (Parakeet v2) at 11.7%/359x — i.e. Apple is competitive on accuracy, not fastest, free. A later 2026-04-28 multi-engine benchmark (dicta.to, 13k clips, 5 languages) has Apple *winning* clean-speech WER in French/German/Italian (as low as 4.0% Italian) while WhisperKit leads English/Spanish by a small margin — methodology not published, treat as directional only.
- **WhisperKit (Argmax, open-source Swift/CoreML, MIT)**: Whisper models running on ANE via CoreML. Streaming, word-level timestamps, VAD ship in the OSS tier. **Custom vocabulary / contextual biasing is Pro-only** (paid, proprietary) — the OSS SDK's stated position is "transcription only... for word-level alignment plus real-time speaker labels, Argmax Pro is the supported path." iOS 17+ minimum, so it does not require iOS 26.
- **whisper.cpp (ggml-org, C/C++, MIT)**: the OG on-device Whisper runtime. Metal + Core ML (ANE) support on Apple Silicon, ANE encoder path claimed >3x faster than CPU-only. Quantized models (Q5/Q4) cut size ~2-4x (base 74MB→~18MB int8; large-v3 1.5GB→~1.1GB q5_0 "negligible WER loss" — vendor claim, unverified). This is the underlying engine WhisperKit and most "Whisper on iPhone" apps wrap; iPhone-specific RTF/battery numbers were not found in the mid-2026 material I could retrieve (most benchmarks target Mac/Apple Silicon desktop, not iPhone thermal envelope — **notable gap, see benchmark plan**).
- **Moonshine v2 (Moonshine AI / Useful Sensors, public release 2026-02-23, arXiv 2602.12241)**: streaming-first architecture, no fixed 30s window (unlike Whisper's chunking), claimed <200ms latency via encoder-state caching (avoids full recompute per chunk — architecturally the closest fit to our append-only cursor need). Tiny model 26MB/12.66% WER, Tiny-Streaming 34MB/12.00% WER, Base 58MB/10.07% WER (self-reported). English-centric; multilingual maturity unclear. No iOS-specific deployment story found (Python/HF-first); would need a from-scratch CoreML or Rust port — **real integration cost, not off-the-shelf on iOS**.
- **NVIDIA Parakeet / Canary (NeMo)**: Parakeet TDT 0.6B v2 reports 6.05% WER, "50x faster than alternatives" (NVIDIA's own framing, presumably vs. GPU-class comparisons — not iPhone-relevant on its face). On-device Apple path exists only via third-party CoreML ports: **FluidAudio** (Apache-2.0 Swift framework) converts Parakeet + pyannote diarization to CoreML/ANE; Argmax Pro also ships a Parakeet-based path (paid). No first-party Apple or NVIDIA iOS SDK. NVIDIA's own edge work (Nemotron Speech Streaming, arXiv 2604.14493) targets CPU-constrained edge but is not shown running on iOS.
- **Vosk (alphacep, Apache-2.0)**: mature offline toolkit, small models (~50MB) for mobile, iOS bindings exist. Consistently characterized in 2026 sources as *behind* Whisper-class models under noisy/difficult conditions ("Whisper is enormously more accurate than Vosk" — comparison-site framing, low rigor but directionally consistent with its 2019-era Kaldi lineage). Supports vocabulary customization. Given our noisy-jobsite requirement, treat as a fallback/legacy option, not a contender.
- **FluidAudio (Apache-2.0, Swift/CoreML)**: not a model but a deployment framework worth flagging separately — ports Parakeet (STT) and pyannote-derived diarization to ANE. Diarization: 0.017 RTF (60x real-time) on M1, DER 22.14% vs desktop Pyannote 3.0's 17.0% (accuracy tradeoff for the speed). This is the most concrete on-device **diarization** story found; still Mac-centric in published benchmarks, iPhone numbers unconfirmed.

No credible engine beyond this set surfaced in mid-2026 search (no new entrant displacing Whisper-family/Apple/Moonshine/Parakeet as of this research date).

## Answers to the eight questions

**1. Accuracy on noisy/outdoor/accented speech.** No engine has a published construction-site-specific benchmark. Generalizable data: Whisper large-v3 clean WER ~2.7%, real-world (meetings/podcasts) ~8-12%, with noise adding +5-15% WER, accents +5-10%, phone-mic vs studio +5-10%, overlapping speakers +5-10% (aggregated from 2026 comparison-site synthesis — **medium confidence, no primary source with raw numbers**). Independent-ish estimate: real-world English WER for Whisper large-v3 ≈10.6%. Apple's model and Parakeet don't have outdoor/noise-specific numbers either. **Bottom line: nobody has told us what happens on a windy job site with a nail gun in the background — this is squarely a "measure it ourselves" item**, not a literature answer.

**2. On-device performance (RTF/memory/battery/model size), iPhone-class.** Best iPhone-specific data is absent; nearly everything published is Mac/Apple-Silicon-desktop (M1–M5), not iPhone thermal/battery envelope. Whisper.cpp on Mac: RTF ~0.08-0.14 (7-12x real-time) with Metal; thermal throttling reported after 10-15 min sustained large-v3 use *on laptops*, mitigated by smaller models/threads cap. WhisperKit ANE path is the one actually iPhone-shipping (17+); Argmax's benchmark numbers above are Mac-only though. Model size options are wide: Moonshine Tiny 26MB, whisper.cpp base int8 ~18MB up to large-v3 q5 ~1.1GB, Parakeet 0.6B params (CoreML-converted size not confirmed). Apple SpeechAnalyzer's model size is invisible to us — it's a system asset, not app-bundled, so it doesn't count against IPA size but its RTF/battery on an actual iPhone during an hour-long call is **undocumented anywhere I found**. **This whole dimension is the single biggest open question for a benchmark plan — see below.**

**3. Streaming/incremental semantics — the append-only question.** This is the most product-critical finding:
   - **Apple SpeechAnalyzer**: explicit **volatile vs. finalized** result model — volatile (real-time, revisable) results are shown then *replaced* by finalized results as more audio arrives. This is **not append-only by default**: the API is designed around the assumption that consumers re-render volatile text. One community report (SwiftCaptionTesting notes, 2026-06) flags a bug/quirk where volatile results can duplicate the tail of a just-finalized segment. **To get an append-only stream from SpeechAnalyzer, we'd only consume `.finalized` results and discard volatile ones** — trading latency (finalization lag) for correctness of our cursor invariant. This is workable but means our "live extraction" is live-ish, not truly live.
   - **whisper.cpp / WhisperKit**: fundamentally a chunked/windowed decoder (30s windows in vanilla Whisper). Segment-level results are typically stable once emitted per chunk (no re-decoding of prior chunks unless using rolling-context tricks), which is closer to append-only *if you architect the chunking yourself* — but naive sliding-window implementations can re-transcribe overlap regions, producing near-duplicate or revised text at chunk boundaries. This needs explicit engineering (chunk-boundary dedup) either way; it's not a free append-only guarantee, it's an emergent property of how you drive the API.
   - **Moonshine v2**: streaming-first, encoder-state caching, described as reusing prior computation rather than restarting — architecturally the best fit for incremental, likely-append-only emission, but **not proven on our platform** (no iOS deployment found) and no explicit "does it ever revise a prior token" statement located.
   - **Net**: none of the three ships a documented, guaranteed-append-only contract. Apple's is explicitly two-tier (volatile+final) and we'd restrict to finalized-only. Whisper-family needs us to own chunk-boundary logic. Moonshine is theoretically cleanest but unvalidated on-device here. **Recommend: design the Rust cursor to tolerate a bounded "finalize" step (accept only finalized/final segments, buffer volatile ones client-side without feeding the cursor) rather than assuming any engine hands us pure append-only text natively.**

**4. Contextual biasing surface — this is the crux for our vocabulary→STT loop.**
   - **iOS SFSpeechRecognizer `contextualStrings`**: documented cap ~100 phrases (matches our ≤100-term vocabulary design point almost exactly — good product fit), substantially improves domain-term/proper-noun recognition per multiple sources, but is **an SFSpeechRecognizer-era feature**. SpeechAnalyzer (iOS 26) **does not have an equivalent yet** — multiple 2026 sources explicitly state SpeechAnalyzer lacks Custom Vocabulary and call this "a regression" for apps that depended on it. This is a hard constraint: **if we go SpeechAnalyzer, we currently have no first-party contextual biasing surface**, undermining our stated novelty. Worth an explicit Apple Developer Forums / radar check before committing (not found conclusively in this search pass — recommend direct verification, this is the single most decision-relevant unknown).
   - **Whisper-family (`initial_prompt`)**: mechanically mismatched for biasing — the prompt slot expects "prior transcript context," not a keyword list; last-224-tokens-only window means earlier vocabulary terms get diluted; using it as a hotword list induces **hallucinations** (documented: up to 86% of insertions under masking are repetition-loop hallucinations in one paper). A better-fit technique exists — **prefix-trie/logit-biasing hotword decoding** (a whisper.cpp GitHub issue #1979 tracks exactly this ask; academic work — arXiv 2410.18363, 2508.17796 — shows trie-based biasing gives 19-22% B-WER reduction on rare/hotwords) — but this is **not a shipped whisper.cpp feature**, it's a research technique we'd have to implement against whisper.cpp's decoder internals ourselves. Real engineering cost, real payoff.
   - **Argmax Pro / FluidAudio-class custom vocabulary**: reportedly matches cloud-API-level keyword accuracy, but Pro is paid/proprietary — conflicts with our fully-on-device + (presumably) cost-conscious posture, and licensing terms weren't fully surfaced.
   - **Fine-tuning**: ruled out per brief; also impractical for a curated, frequently-changing 100-term list per user.
   - **Net for the "novelty" claim**: our vocabulary→biasing loop is real innovation *if* we do the biasing work ourselves against whisper.cpp (trie/logit-bias) — Apple's platform doesn't currently offer an equivalent on SpeechAnalyzer, and the old contextualStrings mechanism is tied to the API we're trying to move past.

**5. Background execution with screen locked, hour+.** `UIBackgroundModes: audio` + an active `AVAudioSession` (category `.record` or `.playAndRecord`) is the standard mechanism keeping an app alive to keep capturing audio while backgrounded/locked — this is well-established iOS behavior, confirmed by multiple "AI note-taker" apps (Wave, Voice Recorder, Transcribe AI) explicitly marketing hour-long, screen-locked, unlimited-length recording as a feature, so **the capture side is solved and shipping today** (2026) by comparable apps. Two governance risks found: (a) the mic recording indicator (red pill / Dynamic Island) cannot be suppressed — expected, not a blocker; (b) App Store review has in the past rejected apps declaring `audio` background mode without an obviously audio-continuous use case — we should make sure our review notes/justification are airtight (dictation apps have gotten flak before). On the *recognition* side: SFSpeechRecognizer's on-device mode was already reported as removing the ~1-minute ceiling for *on-device* (not server) recognition even pre-iOS-26 — the 1-minute cap was mainly a server-request-based-recognition limit; SpeechAnalyzer removes session limits generally and is explicitly positioned for long-form. Neither Apple source confirms *battery/thermal* behavior over a full locked hour — that's unverified and platform behavior (thermal throttling of the Neural Engine) could differ meaningfully between "record raw audio and transcribe later" (cheap: just AVAudioSession + disk write) vs. "transcribe continuously in the background for an hour" (expensive: sustained NE/CPU use while backgrounded, unclear if iOS lets a backgrounded app sustain that for 60 min without being killed for excessive background CPU — **not found, must test**).

**6. Rust-side vs platform-side.**
   - Binding maturity: `whisper-rs` (tazz4843, MIT, actively maintained per a 2026-03-12 0.16.0 release) is the mainstream Rust binding to whisper.cpp; supports CUDA/ROCm (irrelevant to us) plus the underlying whisper.cpp CPU/Metal paths. No UniFFI-specific precedent was found for whisper-rs — we'd be first to wire it through UniFFI to Swift, though UniFFI's general pattern (Rust owns state behind an `Arc`/actor, exposes async methods, calls back into Swift via UniFFI callback interfaces for streaming) is documented and used in production-adjacent examples (Phi-3-on-iOS-via-Rust, general "Rust in iOS audio app" writeups). Feasible, not turnkey — expect real integration time on the FFI/callback-streaming boundary, and audio-callback-to-Rust-inference architectures specifically warn that going through FFI from a real-time audio render callback is fragile (recommendation from cp3.io: keep the *audio capture* in Swift/AVAudioEngine, hand buffers to Rust off the real-time thread, not call Rust directly inside the render callback).
   - Licensing: whisper.cpp MIT, WhisperKit MIT (OSS tier), FluidAudio Apache-2.0, Vosk Apache-2.0, Moonshine — check license before adoption (not confirmed in this pass, historically Apache-2.0/MIT-ish for Useful Sensors releases but **verify**). All permissive, no GPL/restrictive blockers found for the credible on-device candidates. Argmax Pro and any NVIDIA NeMo/Riva commercial tier are the exceptions (proprietary/paid).
   - Model distribution / App Store size: whisper.cpp models (quantized, tens of MB to ~1GB) would need to ship as bundled resources or on-demand-downloaded assets — counts against app size / requires on-demand resources plumbing either way. Apple SpeechAnalyzer's models are *not* our problem — system-managed, downloaded once system-wide, shared across apps, zero IPA cost. This is a real, concrete point in Apple's favor for v1 simplicity, independent of accuracy.
   - **Net**: platform-side (SpeechAnalyzer) is architecturally the path of least resistance (no bundling, no FFI-streaming-audio complexity, Apple owns background-execution correctness) but forecloses biasing (today) and Android portability entirely. Rust-side (whisper.cpp via whisper-rs/UniFFI) is more engineering (audio pipeline design, model distribution, FFI streaming) but buys biasing control, cross-platform hedge (Android later shares the Rust core), and no dependency on Apple's roadmap for custom vocabulary ever shipping on SpeechAnalyzer.

**7. Timestamps + speaker awareness.** Word/segment timestamps: available in WhisperKit (word-level, documented), in whisper.cpp (segment-level natively, word-level via cross-attention alignment techniques, widely used), and Apple's Speech framework has historically exposed timing metadata per result (not independently reconfirmed for SpeechAnalyzer specifically in this pass — reasonable to assume continuity but **verify**). This satisfies our future audio-scrubbing UI need regardless of engine choice — not a differentiator. Diarization: no first-party on-device diarization in Apple's stack found; the credible on-device path is **FluidAudio** (Apache-2.0, pyannote-derived, ANE-optimized, DER ~22% vs desktop's ~17%) — usable but Mac-proven more than iPhone-proven, and DER 22% is meaningfully worse than server-grade; treat as nice-to-have exactly as briefed, not v1-critical.

**8. Battery/thermal mitigation patterns for hour-long capture.** The dominant pattern among comparable shipping apps (Wave, Voice Recorder, Transcribe AI, Jamie) is **record-then-transcribe**: capture raw audio continuously (cheap — just AVAudioSession + disk), and defer the actual STT compute to after the recording ends (one app markets "1 hour of audio transcribed in 30 seconds" post-hoc). This directly conflicts with our "streaming-ish transcript for live extraction" requirement — the shipping precedent for long recordings is explicitly *not* continuous live transcription; it's cheap capture + batch transcription. A **hybrid** is plausible and is implicitly what SpeechAnalyzer's volatile/finalized model and whisper.cpp's chunking both support: run STT continuously but in coarser segments (e.g., re-transcribe/emit every N seconds of buffered audio rather than truly per-frame), trading a few seconds of latency for materially less sustained NE/CPU duty-cycle than a naive fully-continuous streaming decoder. No vendor publishes a specific chunked-vs-continuous battery delta for iPhone; this is squarely a **build-and-measure** item, not a literature answer.

## Scorecard vs. our design intent

**At frontier / good fit:** on-device-only requirement is table stakes now, well-supported by all serious candidates (Apple, WhisperKit, whisper.cpp, Moonshine all fully on-device); the ≤100-term vocabulary design point matches iOS's own historical contextualStrings cap almost exactly, suggesting our number wasn't arbitrary — it's converging with what these systems can actually use; record-then-transcribe is the proven shipping pattern for hour-long capture, meaning our live-extraction ambition is the genuinely novel/harder part, not the recording part.

**Behind frontier / open gaps:** (1) no engine ships a documented append-only streaming contract — we must engineer around volatile/finalized (Apple) or chunk-boundary dedup (Whisper-family) ourselves; (2) SpeechAnalyzer's lack of a custom-vocabulary equivalent is a real regression vs. the API it replaces and directly threatens our biasing novelty if we go platform-side; (3) essentially zero iPhone-specific (as opposed to Mac) battery/RTF/thermal data exists in public sources for any engine during hour-long sustained use — this is an industry-wide documentation gap, not something we're missing by not reading hard enough; (4) construction-site-specific noise/accent WER doesn't exist anywhere — nobody has benchmarked "outdoor + power tools + hard hat + regional accent."

**Deliberately worth accepting as unknowns for now:** diarization (nice-to-have, FluidAudio exists if we want it later); Android story (out of scope for v1, but the Rust-side path keeps the door open cheaply since the Rust core would be shared).

## Recommendation: three viable architectures

### Option A — Apple SpeechAnalyzer, platform-side, v1-only-iOS
Use `SpeechTranscriber` directly from Swift, consume `.finalized` segments only (discard volatile) to satisfy the append-only cursor invariant, run under `UIBackgroundModes: audio` for the locked-screen hour+ session.
- **Costs:** no contextual-biasing surface today (must re-verify — see benchmark plan item 1) — undermines the vocabulary-loop novelty unless we find an undocumented workaround or Apple ships one; iOS 26+ only (cuts off pre-iOS-26 devices, acceptable if Murmur is greenfield); zero Android path — the moment we want Android we're starting STT from scratch there; we are fully dependent on Apple's model quality/roadmap with no fallback.
- **Buys us:** dramatically simpler engineering (no FFI streaming boundary, no model bundling/download, no thermal-tuning of our own decoder loop); Apple owns background-execution correctness and battery optimization; free; best-in-class WER on several languages per early 2026 benchmarks.

### Option B — whisper.cpp Rust-side via whisper-rs + UniFFI, cross-platform hedge
Rust `stt` crate wraps whisper-rs, audio captured in Swift/AVAudioEngine and handed to Rust off the real-time thread, chunked decoding with explicit boundary-dedup logic for append-only emission, custom trie/logit-bias hotword decoding built against whisper.cpp internals for our vocabulary loop.
- **Costs:** genuinely more engineering — FFI streaming design, model distribution (on-demand resources or bundled, tens-to-hundreds of MB), building hotword biasing ourselves (not shipped anywhere), owning thermal/battery tuning (chunk size, model size selection) with zero iPhone-specific benchmark literature to lean on.
- **Buys us:** real contextual biasing control (the trie/logit-bias approach has published 19-22% B-WER wins on hotwords) — this is what makes the vocabulary→STT novelty claim actually true; MIT license, no paid tier; shared Rust core means Android STT is "port whisper-rs to Android," not "start over"; no dependency on Apple's feature roadmap.

### Option C — Hybrid: Apple SpeechAnalyzer for v1 ship, whisper.cpp Rust path built in parallel as the biasing/cross-platform answer
Ship v1 on Option A for time-to-market and background-execution simplicity (matches record-then-transcribe precedent risk profile), while building the `stt` Rust crate against whisper.cpp as a Plan 06b follow-on specifically to unlock the vocabulary-biasing novelty and Android. Requires the Rust core's transcript-consumption interface to be engine-agnostic from day one (append-only cursor, finalize semantics) so swapping the backend later doesn't touch the extraction pipeline.
- **Costs:** two integration paths to maintain conceptually (even if only one ships at a time); requires disciplined interface design up front to avoid the swap becoming a rewrite.
- **Buys us:** de-risks the v1 ship date without abandoning the differentiated bet; the Rust core cursor gets designed for engine-agnosticism regardless, which is good hygiene even if we never swap.

**My read:** Option C, with a strong bias toward not letting "v1 on Apple" become permanent — the contextual-biasing gap on SpeechAnalyzer is a direct hit to the feature the product is claiming as novel, and record-then-transcribe (the proven pattern) already tells us live-extraction-while-recording is the harder, less-trodden path regardless of which STT engine sits underneath it. If forced to pick one architecture for the whole roadmap today rather than a staged bet, I'd lean **Option B**, because the vocabulary loop is stated as our core differentiator and Option A currently cannot deliver it.

## Benchmark plan — what to measure ourselves before committing

Because iPhone-specific (not Mac) data is almost entirely absent from public sources, treat the following as required, not optional, before Plan 06 locks an architecture:

1. **Verify SpeechAnalyzer biasing status directly**: check current Apple Developer docs/forums for any contextual-biasing API added to `SpeechTranscriber` since WWDC25 (this space moves fast; re-check at write time, not from this doc).
2. **Test audio corpus**: record 3-5 real GC site-walk sessions (~20-60 min each) covering: quiet interior walkthrough, active construction noise (power tools/generators), outdoor wind, at least one non-native-English-accented speaker, and deliberate use of trade jargon/proper nouns from a sample vocabulary list.
3. **Measure per engine (Apple SpeechAnalyzer finalized-only; whisper.cpp small/base quantized via WhisperKit or whisper-rs prototype)**: WER against a hand-transcribed reference; wall-clock RTF on an actual iPhone (not simulator, not Mac); battery percentage consumed over one full hour with screen locked; whether the app is still alive/foregrounded-audio-active at minute 60 (background-kill risk); peak/sustained memory.
4. **Measure biasing lift concretely**: same audio, with and without vocabulary injection (contextualStrings if still available for comparison baseline, and our whisper.cpp trie-biasing prototype), on the jargon/proper-noun segments specifically — this is the number that actually validates or kills the novelty claim.
5. **Measure append-only-ness empirically**: log every emitted segment/token with timestamps for both engines, confirm whether any previously-emitted (finalized) text is ever altered downstream — this directly validates or invalidates the Rust cursor's assumption.
6. **Decision gate**: if Apple's finalized-only stream + zero biasing still beats whisper.cpp+biasing by enough WER margin on our actual noisy corpus, that's a real signal to reconsider prioritization — don't assume the novelty bet pays for itself without checking.
